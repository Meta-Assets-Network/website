## Underlying Support for Virtual-Real Integration: Collaborative Design of Dual Architecture of Technology and Institution

The virtual-real integration of MA Chain is supported by the technical interconnection of cross-chain relay and institutional confirmation of RWA on-chaining. This dual architecture design is the core uniqueness of MA Chain's virtual-real integration.

### Cross-Chain Relay Protocol: Value Bridge for Full-Ecosystem Technical Interconnection

MA Chain builds a heterogeneous cross-chain relay system compatible with technical interfaces of public chains, consortium chains and Web2 centralized systems. Through three core components: light client verification, Verifiable Cross-Chain Message (VCM) and cross-chain settlement contract, it realizes technical interconnection and seamless value circulation of different ecosystems, different from the limitation of traditional cross-chain solutions that only focus on the blockchain ecosystem:

- **Multi-protocol compatible cross-chain relay nodes**: Deploy decentralized cross-chain relay nodes supporting protocol adaptation of EVM, non-EVM public chains and Web2 systems, realizing standardized transmission of cross-ecosystem data, assets and instructions, ensuring cross-domain intercommunication of real-economy data of Web2, digital assets of Web3 and physical asset information of real economy;
- **Verifiable cross-chain value transmission**: All cross-chain asset and data circulation is verifiably measured through POCC consensus. Cross-chain transaction hashes and execution results are endorsed and stored on-chain via TSS threshold signatures, avoiding double-spending and data tampering in cross-chain scenarios, ensuring the security and traceability of cross-ecosystem value circulation;
- **Automated cross-chain settlement**: Realize automated settlement of cross-ecosystem value based on on-chain smart contracts, supporting multi-mode adaptation of digital assets, RWA assets and legal currency settlement, solving the pain points of low efficiency and reliance on third-party intermediaries in traditional cross-chain settlement, and realizing real-time settlement of cross-ecosystem circulation of virtual and real assets.

### RWA Computing Power Assetization System: Trusted Anchoring of Physical Assets to the Virtual World

Taking computing power assets as the core entry point, MA Chain builds a full-process verifiable RWA on-chaining system, transforming core physical assets in the real economy such as computing power equipment, industrial computing power and data centers into on-chain measurable, tradable and composable MetaAssets, becoming the core value anchor for virtual-real integration, different from the singleness of traditional RWA on-chaining that only focuses on financial assets:

- **Standardized value mapping of physical assets**: Quantify the value of heterogeneous computing power physical assets in the real economy through Normalized Capacity Score (NCS), complete on-chain confirmation with EIP-721 compatible Real-NFTs, realize 1:1 anchoring between the value of physical assets and on-chain digital assets, ensuring on-chain assets are backed by real physical assets;
- **Full-process institutional verification of RWA on-chaining**: Physical asset on-chaining requires five steps: "material submission - custodial registration - measurement verification - community challenge - TSS signature authorization". Each step generates traceable hash evidence and uploads it to the chain, institutionally eliminating false physical asset mapping and ensuring the authenticity and legality of RWA on-chaining;
- **Real-time synchronization of physical assets and on-chain asset status**: Relying on TEE/TPM remote attestation, offline custodial audit and on-chain smart contracts, realize real-time synchronization between the status of physical assets (operation, suspension, retirement) and on-chain RWA asset status. Status changes require TSS threshold signature endorsement, avoiding the virtual-real disconnection of "valid on-chain assets, invalid off-chain assets".
