## POCC Consensus: Verifiable Anchoring of Computing Power Asset Value, Consolidating the Value Foundation

POCC consensus abandons the pure capital anchoring (PoS) or useless computing power consumption (PoW) model, anchoring value on auditable, custodial and measurable real physical computing power assets. It achieves verifiability of computing power value through dual technical and institutional design, strongly binding on-chain value to the actual utility of physical assets.

- **Verifiable Measurement of Computing Power Capacity**: Through triple verification mechanisms of TEE/TPM remote attestation, on-site audit by custodians, and community challenges, standardized measurement is conducted on multi-dimensional indicators of physical computing power devices such as CPU/GPU computing power, video memory, bandwidth, and online rate to generate Normalized Capacity Score (NCS). The measurement data is hashed and uploaded to the chain, and traceable certification materials are retained off-chain, ensuring the authenticity and immutability of computing power capacity;
- **Verifiable Composition of Consensus Weight**: Consensus weight is formed by combining Computing Capacity (CU) with slashable staking. The weight calculation process is transparently executed through on-chain contracts, and the weight result of each calculation is endorsed and uploaded to the chain via TSS threshold signature. Any node can verify the compliance of weight sources and the actual contribution of computing power assets, avoiding value anchoring failure caused by capital concentration;
- **Verifiable Implementation of Computing Power Utility**: POCC consensus binds the value of computing power assets to actual service utility. Computing power devices realize value realization by participating in network tasks and computing power rental. Their contribution records are retained through off-chain aggregation, TSS signature endorsement, and on-chain submission of summaries, ensuring that computing power value is generated based on real physical services rather than mere on-chain bookkeeping, realizing "value generated by utility, utility verifiable and traceable".

## Real-NFTs: Verifiable Binding of Physical Computing Power Assets and On-Chain Value, Realizing Unique Ownership Confirmation

Based on the EIP-721 standard, MA Chain creates Real-NFTs as the only on-chain digital mapping carrier of real physical computing power assets, breaking through the limitation that traditional NFTs only represent digital rights. It realizes "each Real-NFT corresponds to one/a group of custodial physical computing power devices, and each NFT ownership change corresponds to the legal transfer of physical asset rights". Its core value lies in the verifiable binding between physical assets and on-chain NFTs, ensuring the consistency of on-chain value and off-chain physical asset rights.

- **Full-Link Verifiability in Minting**: The minting of Real-NFTs requires five steps: "device material submission - foundation custodial registration - off-chain measurement verification - community challenge period review - TSS threshold signature authorization". Each step generates traceable hash evidence and uploads it to the chain. The challenge period provides a window for the community and third parties to verify asset authenticity, eliminating false computing power asset mapping from the source and ensuring that minted Real-NFTs are backed by real physical assets;
- **Verifiable Traceability of Asset Information**: The tokenURI of Real-NFTs points to off-chain auditable metadata of computing power devices, including core information such as device configuration, custodial certification, measurement evidence hash, validity period, and status. Only hashes and status summaries are retained on-chain, ensuring data privacy while realizing "on-chain hash verification, off-chain detail traceability". Any entity can verify the real information of physical assets corresponding to NFTs through on-chain hashes;
- **Verifiable Synchronization of Status Changes**: The status of Real-NFTs (Active/Suspended/Revoked/Retired) is bound in real time to the actual status of offline computing power devices. Changes in device online rate, custodial status, and firmware status trigger on-chain NFT status updates. Update operations require TSS threshold signature endorsement and on-chain recording, ensuring the verifiable consistency between on-chain NFT status and actual physical asset status, avoiding value disconnection of "valid on-chain assets, invalid off-chain assets".
